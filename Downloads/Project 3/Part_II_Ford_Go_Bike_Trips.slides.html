Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I want to look at the type of people who make use of the bike services and determine the possible reason they make use of the bike, either for work or leisure. The main features of interest are user_type, member_birth_year, and member_gender.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset initially contained 183412 samples and 16 features but was reduced to 174952 samples and 16 features after cleaning the data. The dataset consists of features such as duration of ride per sec, start_station, end_station, and end_station_latitude among others but my explanation will revolve around features such as user_type, member_birth_year, member_gender and duration_sec.

Distribution of Member Gender by Birth Year¶

There are three types of member gender in the dataset which are Male, Female and Other. Other is assumed to stand for users who are non-binary or whose gender was not disclosed.

The highest number of gender is male with 7000plus users, followed by females of over 2500 and over 200 other gender. Although there is a significant difference in the number of users per gender, the highest number of users by birth year in each group falls between 1980 to 1997 which implies that the majority of the users are youths and adults.

Member Gender vs Number of User who use bike for all trips.¶

Although most users are subscribers, the bike is not their main means of transportation. Given that the majority of the users are adults and youths as seen in the previous visualizations, one may conclude that it serves as a means of exercise and leisure.

Distribution of Subscribers and Customers Birth Year, Gender, and Duration of Ride¶

The ride duration is not determined by gender or user type but by age. All users with the same birth year had a very high correlation with the duration of seconds they ride the bike.

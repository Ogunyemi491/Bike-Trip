Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This data set contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue. There are 21 features and 10866 samples available in the dataset. The features are 'id', 'imdb_id', 'popularity', 'budget', 'revenue', 'original_title', 'cast', 'homepage', 'director', 'tagline', 'keywords', 'overview','runtime', 'genres', 'production_companies', 'release_date', 'vote_count', 'vote_average', 'release_year', 'budget_adj','revenue_adj

Question(s) for Analysis¶

In this project we will be analysing movies and how features such as budget and vote count determines revenue, and popularity.

Conclusions¶

1: Movies with high budgets generally have a high revenue compared to movies with low budgets. The higher the budget, the higher the revenue tends to be. Note: In this dataset, low_budget movies have more samples than high_budget movies.

2: Although movies with high vote counts are more popular. High vote count movies are more popular than movies with a low vote count.

3: Movies budget also contributes to the popularity of the movie. The average of the movies with high budgets is more popular than the mean of movies with low budgets.

Limitation¶

1: The sample size of the low budget movies is far greater than the available sample for high budgets movies and I think it will be more reasonable for the samples to be of the same proportion or at least very close.
